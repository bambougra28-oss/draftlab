## Patterns observed

1. **Engage-tool distribution skewed toward soft-single** : ~50% of champions, vs ~20% hard-aoe and ~30% none. Reflects the LoL design: most CC is single-target.

2. **HyperCarry flag is rare** (~10% of champions): Aatrox-tier scalers don't qualify; reserved for true 1v9 late carries (Kog'Maw, Vayne, Veigar, Tristana, Kassadin, Vladimir, Smolder, Sion, Senna, Karthus, Aurelion Sol, Kayle, Jax, Dr. Mundo, Nasus, Jinx).

3. **Range bucket distribution** : ~55% melee, ~30% ranged-short, ~15% ranged-long. The ranged-long bucket is concentrated in artillery mages + a handful of ADCs (Caitlyn, Jhin, Senna, Varus, Ashe).

4. **Damage type with secondary** : 12 hybrids identified (Akali, Kai'Sa, Katarina, Kog'Maw, Senna, Shaco, Teemo, Yunara, Varus, Corki, Ezreal). Pure-AP and pure-AD dominate.

5. **GamePlanHints applied conservatively** : assigned only when the champion is widely recognized as signature for an archetype. Most champions left without hints (the M4.2 classifier infers from dimensions).

## Points d'attention pour M4.2

- The Game Plan Classifier should expect ~85% of comps to have a clear primary archetype (>40% confidence). Comps with all-soft-engage + no-hard-aoe risk being ambiguous.
- Range distribution affects Siege weighting heavily — long-range mages dominate Siege scoring.
- Tank-AD vs Tank-AP labeling is fluid for some (Malphite, Alistar, Tahm Kench): the `damageType` choice in their tags reflects current pro meta but may need recalibration on patch shifts.
- Form-changing champions (Jayce, Gnar, Nidalee, Elise, Kayn, Yunara) have notes flagging the form they're tagged on. M4.2 may need form-aware logic in the future.
